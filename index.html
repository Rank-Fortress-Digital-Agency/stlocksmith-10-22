What Is a Bump-Proof Lock?


A bump-proof lock is a locking mechanism designed to resist “bumping,” a technique that burglars use to break into homes. Essentially, all a burglar needs is a blank key and a hammer to break into a house with a conventional lock. With a bump-proof lock, however, they won’t be able to do this even if they have the key. That’s because bump-proof locks are designed so the pins inside the lock cannot be moved out of place using brute force.

If you’re looking for a way to improve the security of your home, then investing in a bump-proof lock is a great option. It will make it more difficult for burglars to break into your home and give you peace of mind knowing that your home is more secure.

There are a few things to remember when shopping for a bump-proof lock. First, you’ll want to ensure that the lock is certified by an independent testing agency. How Do Bump-Proof Locks Work?


Bump-proof locks work by using special pins resistant to being moved out of place. Two main pins are used in bump-proof locks: spool pins and mushroom or wards guide pins. Spool pins have grooves cut into them that make it difficult for them to be pushed out of place, while mushrooms or wards show pins have bumps or ridges that make it hard for them to slide out of position.

Most bump-proof locks also have a security plate that covers the pins, making it even more difficult for them to move. This means that if someone does manage to bump the lock, they will still not be able to open it.

You can do a few things to ensure your home is as secure as possible. First, ensure all your doors have good quality, bump-proof locks installed. You should also consider installing a security system and keeping all windows locked. By taking these simple steps, you can help protect your home from burglars.
